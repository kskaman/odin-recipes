Chef John's Roasted Butternut Squash Soup

Description

This roasted butternut squash soup is my top pick for autumn. It's budget-friendly, simple to make, nourishing, and utterly tasty! For a heartier option, consider adding crispy bacon bits or diced ham on top.

Ingredients

Soup:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 cup carrots, sliced
  • 6 garlic cloves, whole and peeled
  • 2 teaspoons kosher salt, or to taste, divided
  • 1 (3 1/2 pound) butternut squash, cut in half lengthwise and seeded
  • 6 cups chicken broth, with extra if necessary
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup
  • 2 tablespoons cider vinegar
  • 1 pinch cayenne pepper
  • 0.5 cup crème fraîche
  • 1 tablespoon fresh chives, chopped

Sage Brown Butter:

  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 6 fresh sage leaves

Steps

  1. Gather all necessary ingredients and preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Prepare the Vegetables: Drizzle olive oil into a large roasting pan. Add the chopped onion, sliced carrots, whole garlic cloves, and a generous pinch of salt. Toss to ensure the vegetables are well-coated with oil. Place the butternut squash halves on top of the vegetables, cut side facing upwards. Sprinkle additional salt over the squash.
  3. Roast in the oven until the squash is soft and easily pierced with a knife, approximately 1 hour. Once done, remove from the oven and let the vegetables cool slightly.
  4. Make the Sage Brown Butter : In a saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Allow it to foam and begin to turn golden. Lower the heat to prevent burning. When the butter reaches a light brown, nutty aroma, remove from heat and quickly stir in the sage leaves.
  5. Combine and Simmer: Scoop the flesh from the roasted squash and add it to a large stockpot along with the roasted vegetables. Po ur in the chicken broth, the sage-infused brown butter, and maple syrup. Place the pot over high heat and bring to a boil. Once boiling, reduce the heat to low and let it simmer gently for 45 to 60 minutes to allow the flavors to meld. Remove from heat afterward.
  6. Using an immersion blender, puree the soup until smooth, about 3 to 4 minutes. For an extra smooth texture, strain the mixture through a fine sieve to eliminate any remaining fibrous pieces.
  7. If the soup is too thick for your liking, gradually add a few tablespoons of water or additional chicken broth until desired consistency is reached. Stir in the cider vinegar, adjust salt if necessary, and add a pinch of cayenne pepper for a subtle heat.
  8. Serve the soup warm, garnished with a spoonful of crème fraîche and a sprinkle of chopped chives.

Chef's Tips

  • Enhance the soup's richness by topping it with crispy bacon bits or diced ham.
  • Adjust the amount of cayenne pepper based on your preferred spice level.
  • For a vegan alternative, replace chicken broth with vegetable broth and use a plant-based crème fraîche substitute.
